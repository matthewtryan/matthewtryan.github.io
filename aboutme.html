About Me

I am Matthew T. Ryan, a Junior Immunology and Infectious Disease major at The Pennsylvania State University. Before this, I graduated from Plymouth-Whitemarsh High School in Plymouth Meeting, Pennsylvania. I was an active participant in many extracurriculars from baseball to the astronomy club. In my senior year, I was an AP scholar and received an award for Distinguished Excellence in Academics. After taking a microbiology class in my Sophomore year of highschool, I fell in love with infectious microbes and wanted to study their interaction with the immune system. Thus, I decided on an Immunology and Infectious Disease major.

Upon arriving at Penn State, I took many general biology, chemistry, and immunology classes. I also had multiple laboratory classes in varying disciplines that provided with me with the experience necessary to be a productive researcher in a laboratory. I received an academic scholarship from the College of Agricultural Sciences in my Freshmen year. I have been on the Dean's List twice and currently maintain a GPA of 3.53/4.00.

In my freshman year, I joined the Penn State Club Floor Hockey (link) Team as a goaltender and became the captain in my Sophomore year. I was also elected as Secretary of the club in my Sophomore year and re-elected in my Junior year following the increased success of the club.

I am a Laboratory Teaching Assistant in Molecular Biology where I am responsible for grading research papers and teaching students proper writing and technical laboratory skills. In my Sophomore year, I was a Lecture Assistant for Introductory Biology and held office hours to discuss past exam questions with students and help them with difficult concepts presented in class.

In my Junior year, I became an undergraduate researcher the Stephenson Laboratory where I am currently exploring pathogen interactions between plants and herbivores and how inbreeding affects the fitness of these organisms.

In the summer of 2017, I will be completing an Undergraduate Research Fellowship at Drexel University where I will be working with a team to construct a research project and present my findings at an end of summer conference.

My short term goals are to be on the Dean's List for the rest of my semesters at Penn State and to publish a research paper before I graduate. In the future, I hope to obtain a Master's Degree in either Microbiology or Global Epidemiology which will allow me to pursue a career working for the Center of Disease Control or the World Health Organization. Here, I would like to help track the spread of infectious diseases and determine the most effective response for epidemics.
